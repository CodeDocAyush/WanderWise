📍 Kerala Paradise

Fondly referred to as "God's Own Country", Kerala is a tropical coastal paradise famous globally for its shimmering emerald backwaters, lush spice plantations, and Ayurvedic wellness practices.

🗺️ Top Places to Explore

Alleppey Backwaters

Rent traditional houseboats called Kettuvallams to float calmly through peaceful palm-fringed canal networks.

Munnar Hills

A mesmerizing, mist-laden high altitude destination blanketed entirely in manicured rolling green tea estates.

Varkala Cliffs

A unique, breathtaking beach layout where dramatic red sedimentary cliffs sit directly adjacent to the Arabian Sea shorelines.

🚌 Local Transport Options

  • State KSRTC Buses: Extremely affordable at roughly ₹50 - ₹200 for long transit connections spanning smoothly between coastal towns.
  • Public Ferry Boats: Only ₹10 - ₹40 per route. It is the most scenic, culturally authentic way to cross major water inlets.

🏛️ Facts & Historical Specialities

  • Kerala is the foundational birthplace of Ayurveda, one of the world's oldest natural holistic medicine systems dating back thousands of years.
  • It consistently ranks number one across India for having the highest regional literacy rate and the highest statistical life expectancy.

🏨 Stay & Lodging Options

Luxury Kettuvallam Houseboats

Est. Cost: ₹6,000 - ₹12,000 / night
Includes specialized floating chefs serving fresh coconut-infused Kerala seafood meals.

Eco Tea-Estate Lodges

Est. Cost: ₹2,500 - ₹4,500 / night
Wooden cabins built straight into high altitude spice valleys of Munnar or Wayanad.
