📍 Jaipur, Rajasthan

Jaipur, the Pink City of India, blends royal heritage with vibrant culture. Known for majestic forts, bustling bazaars, colorful architecture, and traditional crafts, it offers visitors rich history, Rajasthani cuisine, lively festivals, and landmarks like Hawa Mahal, Amber Fort, and City Palace.

🗺️ Top Places to Explore

Amber Fort

The most iconic fort near Jaipur. Massive courtyards, mirror work, tunnels, and hilltop views. Go early morning to avoid heat and crowds.

Hawa Mahal

The “Palace of Winds” with its famous pink honeycomb façade. Best viewed in the morning from nearby cafes across the road.

Jal Mahal

The “Water Palace” situated in the middle of Man Sagar Lake, offering a stunning reflection of the surrounding architecture.

🚌 Local Transport Options

  • Jaipur Metro: A modern and efficient way to navigate the city, with fares starting at around ₹10 (approximately €0.12) for short rides.
  • Jaipur Bike Share: Around ₹50 per day. Ideal for exploring the city at your own pace.

🏛️ Facts & Historical Specialities

  • Jaipur was nicknamed "The City of Light" originally due to its leading foundational role during the historic Age of Enlightenment.
  • There is only one single stop sign placed in the entire city layout—traffic navigates entirely based on right-of-way rules.
